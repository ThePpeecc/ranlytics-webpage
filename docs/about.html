About RANlytics

Why was RANlytics formed?

RANlytics has been founded by a team of IT and telecommunications industry veterans to address the many issues that have impacted and constrained cellular RF testing for many years.

In establishing the company, we started by clearly identifying the key issues associated with RF testing today, and then validating our understanding with operators, installers, vendors, network integrators and facility owners & managers in a number of countries

That process led to the establishment of 8 objectives that act as our guiding principles, and frame everything we do as a company:

  1. Eliminate cellular RF performance and benchmark testing complexity

  2. Dramatically increase the extent and frequency of testing that is possible

  3. Improve the robustness and test equipment and simplify testing – so that engineers can focus on engineering, design & optimisation instead of testing and processing test data

  4. Democratise and simplify access to RF test data – so that value can be extracted from it by business users as well as technical users

  5. Integrate powerful and highly intuitive analysis and prediction tools with test data – so that users can rapidly gain actionable insight into performance and benchmarking data

  6. Dramatically improve the scaling of RF testing – to meet the requirements of small cell and 5G deployments

  7. Minimise friction in every way in which our customers and partners interact with RANlytics – to earn the right to be called a preferred supplier and business partner

  8. Deliver all of the above while significantly lowering testing costs

What does RANlytics do?

The founders have spent over 6 years developing a complete testing solution architecture that combines custom-built data collection hardware that is far more robust and reliable than legacy equipment, with powerful, highly scalable cloud-based data processing and analytics systems.

The result is a portfolio of cellular RF testing solutions that will continuously evolve and expand. Our solution roadmap already includes plans for tools and functions that will further improve our customers’ ability to extract value from RF testing, analysis and predictive modelling in radically new ways.

We also realised that it wasn’t sufficient to just innovate technically if we are to meet all of our objectives. We also reviewed how legacy tool vendors and IT companies sell and supply their products and services to customers, and identified how the underlying business model could be improved to benefit our customers.

As an example, we have built RANlytics to be an efficient, low-cost business from our very first day. We forego expensive offices, marketing programs and direct salespeople, and instead promote our company and solutions online, and work through hand-picked distribution partners. Our subscription access model is simple and cost-effective. The result is that we can pass considerable cost savings on to our customers.

Who are the RANlytics principals?

Brad McLean

Chief Technology Officer

After graduating with a bachelor of Micro Electronic Engineering, Brad began his career in the telecommunications industry; and over the course of the following 20 years worked for many of the titans in the industry in Australia, New Zealand and S.E. Asia.

Brad has increasingly specialised in wireless telecommunications over the past 15 years, and has extensive experience in all facets of RF testing, design and engineering.

Brad founded Entelecom in 2007 to provide the means to deliver innovative thinking to the wireless telecom sector, and has consulted to both operators and many industry vendors.

Brad has driven the development and engineering of all aspects of the RANlytics solution portfolio.

Today, Brad lives in Jakarta, Indonesia.

Keith Sheridan

Chief Executive Officer

Keith has built a number of high-performing businesses, sales & business development teams, partner ecosystems and delivery organisations in the high technology sector over his 30-year career.

He has founded 2 software companies; and has held leadership roles with established software & hardware vendors including Cisco, Accenture, Oracle, and Sun Microsystems, and start-ups including Vignette and the Claremont Technology Group.
